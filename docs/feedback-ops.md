# Feedback Ops

## What counts as launch feedback

Capture feedback when it:

- blocks onboarding
- repeats across accounts
- exposes confusion in setup, pricing, newsletter, or publishing
- changes whether the product feels launch-ready

## Required fields

Every feedback item should have:

- account link when relevant
- short title
- summary with enough context to act
- status
- priority
- owner when known

## Status usage

- `new`: not yet reviewed
- `reviewed`: understood and triaged
- `planned`: accepted into follow-up work
- `closed`: resolved or intentionally not pursued

## Priority usage

- `high`: launch blocker or repeated severe pain
- `medium`: real friction with workarounds
- `low`: non-blocking polish or longer-term idea

## Good feedback item examples

- "Pricing page does not explain beta onboarding vs paid rollout"
- "Newsletter sender setup is unclear for first-time operators"
- "Blocked onboarding because starter pages were not obvious to review"

## Bad feedback item examples

- "User confused"
- "Something with pricing"
- "Need better UX"
